shadow.guidelines

Usage Rules

Follow these rules to keep the shadow system legible and meaningful across the product.

  • Higher z-index always means greater shadow

    Never render a modal with shadow.sm or a card with shadow.xl. Shadow communicates physical height — breaking this contract confuses the visual hierarchy and undermines the user's spatial model of the interface.

  • Do not stack shadows on dark surfaces

    On dark-mode panels (#222222), shadows are nearly invisible. Use border: 1px solid var(--border) alongside a lighter shadow opacity to maintain definition. The design system's dark theme adjusts panel borders to compensate.

  • Use shadow.inner only for recessed elements

    shadow.inner (inset shadow) signals that an element is pressed into the surface — use it for active toggle states, pressed buttons, and inset input wells. Never apply it to floating or elevated surfaces — inset and elevation are mutually exclusive.

  • Focus rings must always be visible at 3px offset

    Both shadow.focus and shadow.focus-error use a 0 0 0 3px spread ring. Never reduce this below 2px — it exists to pass WCAG 2.4.11 Focus Appearance. Combining with a matching border-color change further improves visibility against varied backgrounds.
